## Design Context

### Users
Avid readers standing in a physical bookshop, phone in hand, making fast purchasing decisions in front of a shelf. Context is real-world, on-the-go — not a leisurely desktop browse. The job: scan a shelf, get an intelligent verdict fast, decide whether to buy.

### Brand Personality
Fast. Sharp. Intelligent. Rich (warm, not cold).

NOT cold, clinical, or tech-startup sterile. Should feel like it belongs in the hands of someone who loves books — human, warm, decisive.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Bookshop Intelligence"** — the feeling of a beautiful independent bookshop at evening: warm amber light, dark wood shelves, cream paper. The app is the intelligent layer over that physical world.

- **Theme**: Dark — but WARM dark (near-black with amber undertones), not cold navy
- **Primary accent**: Amber/warm gold — the color of bookshop lamp light
- **Typography**: Condensed editorial display (Barlow Condensed) for decisiveness; clean body for readability
- **Surfaces**: Solid warm surfaces — NO glassmorphism spam. One glass layer max (nav)
- **NO gradient text** — solid colors only. Weight and size do the hierarchy work.
- **Anti-references**: The current cyan/violet neon-on-navy aesthetic. Any SaaS dashboard. Anything that looks like "AI made this."

### Design Principles
1. **Verdict first** — every screen should deliver its answer at a glance, like a confident bookseller
2. **Warmth over cool** — amber over cyan, wood over glass, paper over steel
3. **Condensed = decisive** — tight type signals fast thinking, not cramped space
4. **One accent, used sparingly** — amber earns its place by appearing rarely
5. **Human at every touchpoint** — empty states teach, errors are kind, language sounds like a person
